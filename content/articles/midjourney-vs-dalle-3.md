## Midjourney: The Artist's Tool

**Price:** $10/month (Basic) to $120/month (Mega)  
**Interface:** Discord bot (v5/v6) or web app (alpha)  
**Model:** Midjourney v6.1 (proprietary)

### What Happened

Midjourney feels like working with a hyper-talented artist who doesn't always listen.

**Prompt example:**
> "A minimalist SaaS dashboard, clean white UI, subtle gradients, modern typography, professional â€”ar 16:9"

**Result:** Stunning... but not a dashboard. It gave me an artistic *interpretation* of a dashboard â€” beautiful abstract shapes, perfect composition, but unusable as an actual UI reference.

**Time to usable image:** 2-5 minutes (including re-rolls and variations)

### What I Loved

**1. Aesthetic quality is unmatched**  
Every Midjourney image looks *polished*. Colors are balanced, composition is professional, lighting is cinematic. Even when it gets the prompt wrong, it looks amazing.

**2. Artistic styles are incredible**  
Want a cyberpunk vibe? Film noir? Watercolor? Studio Ghibli? Midjourney nails artistic styles better than any other tool.

**3. Upscaling and variations**  
Midjourney's upscaling (1024x1024 â†’ 2048x2048+) is sharp. The variation system (V1, V2, V3, V4) makes iteration fast.

**4. Community and inspiration**  
The Midjourney Discord is full of incredible art. You can see what others are creating, learn prompting techniques, and get inspired.

### What Annoyed Me

**1. Discord-first is clunky**  
You prompt via Discord bot. Results appear in a channel. Want to find an old image? Good luck scrolling. The web app exists but is still in alpha.

**2. Prompt interpretation is loose**  
Midjourney *interprets* your prompt artistically. This is great for creative work but terrible for "give me exactly what I described."

Example: I asked for "a red button with white text saying SUBMIT." Midjourney gave me a beautiful red abstract shape with artistic white swooshes. Gorgeous. Not a button.

**3. Text rendering is hit-or-miss**  
DALL-E 3 handles text in images way better. Midjourney often garbles text or ignores it entirely.

**4. Expensive for commercial use**  
You need the $30/month Pro plan for commercial rights. The $10 Basic plan is personal use only.

### Verdict: Best for artistic, creative work

If you're making art, mood boards, concept art, or anything where aesthetic > precision, Midjourney is unbeatable. Just don't expect literal accuracy.

**Interface:** ChatGPT or API

### What Happened

DALL-E 3 is the engineer's image generator. It does *exactly* what you ask.

**Same prompt:**
> "A minimalist SaaS dashboard, clean white UI, subtle gradients, modern typography, professional â€”ar 16:9"

**Result:** An actual dashboard UI. Clean layout, readable text, realistic interface elements. Not as artistically stunning as Midjourney, but *usable* as a reference or mockup.

**Time to usable image:** 30-90 seconds (including ChatGPT thinking time)

### What I Loved

**1. Prompt accuracy**  
DALL-E 3 follows instructions *precisely*. If you say "three people, one wearing red," you get three people, one in red. No artistic reinterpretation.

**2. Text rendering**  
This is DALL-E's superpower. It can generate clean, readable text in images. Want a poster with specific headlines? A meme with exact text? DALL-E nails it.

**3. ChatGPT integration**  
Generating images inside ChatGPT means you can iterate conversationally:
- "Make the background darker"
- "Change the person's shirt to blue"
- "Add a subtle shadow"

No re-typing the entire prompt.

**4. Cheaper for casual use**  
$20/month includes ChatGPT Plus + DALL-E 3. You get ~100 images/day. For most users, this is plenty.

### What Annoyed Me

**1. Aesthetic quality is good, not great**  
DALL-E images look *fine*. Professional, usable, clean. But they don't have Midjourney's artistic *wow* factor.

**2. Less artistic style control**  
DALL-E can do styles ("in the style of Monet"), but it's not as nuanced as Midjourney's artistic rendering.

**3. Resolution limits**  
DALL-E maxes out at 1024x1024 (or 1792x1024 for wide). Midjourney can upscale to 2048x2048+ with better detail.

**4. No community inspiration**  
You're generating in ChatGPT. No gallery, no community, no "wow, how did they make that?" learning.

### Verdict: Best for precise, usable images

If you need a specific result â€” mockups, diagrams, text-heavy images, or "exactly what I described" â€” DALL-E 3 is the clear winner.

## Prompt Engineering: What Actually Works

I tested 100+ prompt variations. Here's what I learned.

### Midjourney prompting tips:
1. **Use artistic references:** "in the style of Studio Ghibli" works better than describing details
2. **Aspect ratios matter:** `â€”ar 16:9`, `â€”ar 2:3`, etc. drastically change composition
3. **Weight keywords:** Use `::2` to emphasize (`cinematic lighting::2`)
4. **Remix mode:** Enable to iterate on existing images with new prompts
5. **Version matters:** v6 is more literal, v5 is more artistic

**Example prompt:**
> "a cozy coffee shop, warm lighting, rainy day outside, Studio Ghibli style, detailed, cinematic composition â€”ar 16:9 â€”v 6"

### DALL-E 3 prompting tips:
1. **Be literal:** Describe exactly what you want, no artistic flourishes
2. **Specify text:** "A poster with the text 'LAUNCH SALE' in bold red letters"
3. **Use conversational refinement:** Generate once, then tweak in ChatGPT
4. **Request styles explicitly:** "photorealistic," "flat design," "watercolor painting"
5. **Positioning matters:** "in the center," "on the left," "in the background"

**Example prompt:**
> "A photorealistic image of a modern coffee shop interior. Clean minimalist design, large windows showing rain outside, warm pendant lights, wooden tables. A chalkboard sign on the left says 'FRESH BREW' in white chalk. Wide angle, natural lighting."
