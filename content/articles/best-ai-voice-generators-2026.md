## 1. ElevenLabs: The Voice Cloning King {#elevenlabs}

**Price:** $5/month (Starter), $22/month (Creator), $99/month (Pro)  
**Best for:** Voice cloning, professional narration

### What It Does

ElevenLabs is the **best voice cloning tool** I've tested. Upload 3-10 minutes of clean audio (you reading a script), and it generates a clone that sounds *eerily like you*.

I recorded myself reading for 5 minutes. ElevenLabs trained on it for 10 minutes. The output? **95% accurate**. Same cadence, same inflection, same quirks.

My girlfriend listened to the clone and said, "Why are you talking like a robot?" She thought it was me practicing scripts.

### What I Loved

**1. Cloning is scary-good**  
This is the only tool where my voice clone is indistinguishable from me. Intonation, pacing, breath patterns — all there.

**2. Emotional range**  
You can dial in tone: excited, serious, calm, urgent. The voice actually *sounds* different, not just faster or louder.

**3. Multilingual support**  
Clone your English voice, then generate in 29 languages. My voice speaking fluent Spanish? Wild.

**4. Fast generation**  
30 seconds of audio generated in 3-5 seconds. No waiting.

**5. Commercial rights included**  
Paid plans include full commercial usage. Use clones in products, ads, videos.

### What Annoyed Me

**1. Expensive at scale**  
$5/month gets you 10,000 characters (~8 minutes of audio). If you're doing YouTube weekly, you'll hit the limit fast. Pro ($99/month) gives 500K characters.

**2. Cloning requires clean audio**  
Background noise, music, or interruptions degrade clone quality. You need a decent mic and quiet room.

**3. Pronunciation quirks**  
Rare words or brand names sometimes sound off. You can use phonetic spelling, but it's trial-and-error.

**4. Can't edit generated audio**  
Want to change one word? Regenerate the whole sentence. No inline editing.

### Real Talk

ElevenLabs is **the industry standard for voice cloning**. If you want an AI version of your voice (or a custom character voice), this is the tool.

I use it for product demos and YouTube videos. The time savings are insane — no recording, no retakes, no editing. Just type, generate, done.

**Try ElevenLabs:** [elevenlabs.io](https://elevenlabs.io) (free tier: 10,000 chars/month)

**2. Ultra-realistic voices (stock)**  
PlayHT's pre-built voices (not clones) are excellent. Great for narration if you don't need *your* voice.

**3. Podcast hosting integration**  
Generate voiceovers and publish directly to podcast platforms. Handy for audiobooks or spoken newsletters.

**4. Good API**  
Embed voice generation in apps. I used it to build a "text-to-podcast" feature for a client.

8-15 seconds for 30 seconds of audio (vs ElevenLabs' 3-5). Noticeable when iterating.

**3. Emotional range is limited**  
Can't dial in tone as precisely as ElevenLabs. Voice sounds more flat.

### Real Talk

PlayHT is **best for budget-conscious creators**. If ElevenLabs is too expensive and you can tolerate slightly-lower quality, PlayHT saves money.

## Voice Cloning Tips (Learned the Hard Way)

### 1. Use clean audio for training
- Quiet room (no AC, traffic, or fans)
- Decent mic (USB mic > laptop mic)
- No music or background voices
- Read naturally (don't overact)

### 2. Record 5-10 minutes
- Too little (< 3 min) = mediocre clone
- Too much (> 15 min) = diminishing returns
- 5-10 minutes is the sweet spot

### 3. Vary your tone
- Read some sentences excited, some calm
- Include questions and statements
- Variety = better emotional range

### 4. Iterate on pronunciation
- Rare words or brand names? Spell them phonetically
- Example: "ElevenLabs" → "eleven labs" (with space)

### 5. Always listen before sharing
- AI makes weird mistakes (mispronounced words, odd pacing)
- Regenerate problem sentences
- Don't ship the first take
